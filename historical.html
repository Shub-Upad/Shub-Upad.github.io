Historical AI Techniques

Historical AI Techniques Hub

Where do modern architectures actually come from? This hub traces the ideas that shaped current AI, not as history, but as active material. Every technique gets replicated. Every replication gets a novel intervention. Some assumptions survived scaling. Some broke quietly. Both are worth understanding.

Resources

  • 1950 | Paper

    Computing Machinery and Intelligence

    Turing's landmark framing of machine intelligence and testability, still useful for grounding evaluation discussions.

  • 1958 | Paper

    The Perceptron: A Probabilistic Model for Information Storage

    Early neural classifier framing that exposes both the optimism and constraints of first-wave connectionist thinking.

  • 1969 | Book

    Perceptrons (Minsky and Papert)

    Critical analysis that redirected the field and remains an instructive case study in capability boundaries.

  • 1986 | Paper

    Backpropagation in Deep Networks

    The modern training backbone, essential for understanding where gradient descent still succeeds or fails in 2026 contexts.

  • 1998 | Paper

    Gradient-Based Learning Applied to Document Recognition

    ConvNet milestone connecting representation learning to production-grade OCR and data-driven feature extraction.
